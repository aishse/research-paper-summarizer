**Summary of the Europa Clipper Launch Service Source Selection Statement**  
This document outlines the procurement process for the Europa Clipper mission’s launch service, a NASA initiative to assess Europa’s habitability via a detailed orbital survey. The selection of SpaceX over United Launch Services, LLC (ULS) was based on a risk-adjusted evaluation prioritizing mission suitability over price. Below are the key findings and rationale:

### **Mission Context and Requirements**  
- **Mission Goal**: Europa Clipper aims to determine if Jupiter’s moon Europa could harbor life by analyzing its subsurface ocean and surface features.  
- **Launch Constraints**: Requires a "Heavy-class" vehicle, a 21-day launch window in 2024, and Category 3 certification due to its Class A payload status (high risk).  
- **Procurement Type**: Full and open competition, restricted to U.S. providers (SpaceX and ULS), with no access to the Space Launch System (SLS).

---

### **Evaluation Criteria and Process**  
The evaluation prioritized three factors in this order:  
1. **Mission Suitability** (1,000 total points):  
   - **Technical Approach (700 points)**: Evaluated feasibility of meeting technical requirements, risk mitigation, and compatibility of launch vehicle components.  
   - **Management Approach (250 points)**: Focused on program management, safety, and communication strategies.  
   - **Small Business Utilization (50 points)**: Assessed subcontracting commitments to small businesses.  

2. **Past Performance** (Weighted less than Mission Suitability but more than Price).  
3. **Price** (Based on reasonableness relative to market conditions).

---

### **Key Findings from Vendor Evaluations**  
**SpaceX (Falcon Heavy)**  
- **Mission Suitability**: Highest score. Strengths included:  
  - Flight-correlated analyses and a proven Falcon Heavy configuration (3 successful flights).  
  - Mitigated risks through common launch vehicle design (no major upgrades required).  
- **Weaknesses**:  
  - Gaps in avionics configuration, telemetry formats, and spacecraft integration timelines.  
- **Past Performance**: High confidence rating due to strong track record in NASA/DoD contracts, consistent deadline adherence, and effective anomaly resolution.
